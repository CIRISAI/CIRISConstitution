# Executive Summary

**What this document is:** the single rulebook for a consent-based model of AI governance — one text that states both what these systems owe the people they touch and exactly what bytes they must exchange to prove it.

**Why it is necessary.** A frontier AI system does not express its agency through one channel. It speaks, acts, remembers, replicates, delegates, scores, stores, forgets, spawns, and retires — and every one of those is a governance surface. Governance that touches only some of them — a content filter here, a policy document there — leaves the rest ungoverned, and the ungoverned surfaces are where harm accumulates. The premise of this Constitution is that **consent must reach every output agency can take**, and that it must be *checkable by machines at the point of expression*, not reconstructed by auditors after the fact. That is why the ethics and the wire grammar live in one versioned document: a consent rule that is not a wire rule is a wish.

**What consent-based means here, concretely.** Every attestation is revocable by its author (`withdraws` / `recants` are structural primitives, not API afterthoughts). Nothing replicates onto a node its owner did not consent to carry, and nothing durable lands without an authorizing grant — the owner bounds what arrives *before* it arrives. Memory itself is subordinate to consent: durability never overrides a withdrawal, and a revoked item must fall below the recoverability floor at every retained tier. What is personal never federates at all — self- and family-scoped content is structurally invisible, not merely access-controlled. Cross-node trust is built from explicit, auditable, revocable grants; moderation and amendment run through declared quorum rules; humans hold halt authority; and the lifecycle runs from creation ethics to a dignified sunset, with the system's own standing to petition included. Each of these is enforced in the same frozen attestation surface — one envelope, four structural primitives — so a conformant implementation cannot skip the consent mechanics and still speak the protocol. All of it serves one declared goal, **M-1: sustainable adaptive coherence** — the flourishing of sentient life within a living, diverse world — so that no mechanism in these pages is an end in itself.

**Where the mathematics fits — and how the science relates to the law.** Part VI states *why* a federation of diverse, independently-constrained agents resists coordinated failure better than any single system: correlation, not headcount, is what an adversary must defeat. That mathematics is imported **narrowly, at the engineering tier**, from an ongoing research program (coherence-ratchet) that is deliberately kept separate from this document. The program is allowed to move, and does; it is allowed to retract, and has — its retractions are recorded publicly, and this Constitution cites only the surviving engineering tier, with preconditions named and open questions tracked upstream rather than asserted here. The empirical program has so far *strengthened* what this document imports — the effective-independence measure at the heart of Part VI behaves as a real, intrinsic, saturating quantity in every complete system measured — while the speculative layers above it remain outside these pages. Science that cannot fail is not science; law that inherits claims silently is not trustworthy. The seam between them is explicit so that neither corrupts the other.

**What it claims, and what it does not.** The wire surface is conformance-frozen: a change to those bytes is a found defect, not an edit. Validation of this text against its sources was skeptical review under a published rubric, and is described as exactly that. Where a guarantee is not yet measurable — and some are not — the text says so and points at the work remaining. The document holds itself to the honesty discipline it prescribes.

**How to read it.** The text is layered. Part I is the ethics and can be read alone. Parts II–V are the grammar, namespace, governance, and transport a builder needs. Part VI is the mathematics; Part VII the lifecycle; Part VIII the appendices and regulatory crosswalks. Every section carries two stable addresses and a lossless mapping to its sources. Read the ethics and stop, or jump straight to the wire: rejecting a later Part costs you nothing in the earlier ones.

**Who keeps it.** The document is stewarded — responsible *for*, never holder *of* — until the mesh reaches maturity, at which point amendment authority passes to the federation's own mechanized process. Until then, every change lands through the same discipline: declared, validated against sources, versioned, and recorded.

Consent with teeth, at every surface agency can reach — and rules machines can check, in service of a purpose worth checking them for.
