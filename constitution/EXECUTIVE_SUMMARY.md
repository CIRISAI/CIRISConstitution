# Executive Summary

**What this document is:** the constitution of a decentralized mesh — the rulebook for a consent-based model of governance that reaches every output a frontier AI system's agency can take. It governs the CIRIS Epistemic Web (CEWP): a post-quantum, peer-to-peer network that routes streaming, messages, files, and signed claims directly between the devices people already own — the internet's centralized middle, the handful of intermediaries that today hold both the data and the power to decide what you see, replaced by a network with no platform owner in the middle.

**Why all of it is necessary — the safety thesis.** Safety here is not a property you train into one model's weights and hope holds; it is a property of the *whole* — of many capable agents, people, and organizations whose every consequential decision is independently checkable by anyone. A single super-intelligent system is not something to be aligned but a condition to be prevented: a lone actor answers to no one, and accountability needs more than one party — someone to answer to, and a way to check that cannot be quietly swallowed. So the safety lives in the parts, not in a unified whole; in a diverse federation of the independently-constrained, not a monolith. That is why you cannot take half of this. The ethics without the wire grammar is a wish. The wire grammar without the mesh is a protocol no one runs. The mesh without decentralized, signed governance is just new infrastructure for old capture. **Check the behavior** — a signature, a quorum, a hash-chained audit — rather than trusting what a model reasoned in private. The parts, together and only together, are what is safe.

**Consent that reaches every surface.** A frontier system expresses agency through many channels — it speaks, acts, remembers, replicates, delegates, scores, stores, forgets, spawns, retires — and each is a governance surface. Governance that touches only some leaves the rest to accumulate harm. So consent here is structural and everywhere: every attestation is revocable by its author; nothing replicates onto a node its owner did not consent to carry, and nothing durable lands without an authorizing grant; memory is subordinate to consent, so a withdrawal is honored down to the recoverability floor at every retained tier; what is personal never federates at all; trust between nodes is built from explicit, auditable, revocable grants; humans hold halt authority; and the lifecycle runs from creation ethics to a dignified sunset. All of it is enforced in one frozen attestation surface — one envelope, four structural primitives — so a conformant system cannot skip the consent mechanics and still speak the protocol. A consent rule that is not a wire rule is a wish.

**What the safety assumptions rest on.** The claim that a diverse federation resists coordinated failure better than any single system is not an article of faith — it rests on a specific piece of mathematics (Part VI): what an adversary must defeat is **correlation, not headcount**. A hundred agents that think alike are worth one; independence is the quantity that buys safety, and it is measurable. That mathematics is imported **narrowly, at the engineering tier**, from an ongoing, separately-maintained research program (coherence-ratchet) held to the discipline of real science — it is allowed to fail, and its failures are recorded in the open. This Constitution cites only the surviving engineering tier, names each precondition, and tracks the open questions upstream rather than asserting answers here. As of this writing the empirical program has *strengthened* what the document imports: the effective-independence measure at the core of Part VI behaves as a real, intrinsic, saturating quantity in every complete system it has been measured against — not an artifact of the measurement, and not merely the dimensionality of whatever drives the system. Where a safety guarantee is not yet reducible to a checkable test — and some are not, and are marked as such — the document says so and points at the work remaining. We would rather state a limit than launder a claim.

**Claim and limit.** The wire surface is conformance-frozen: a change to those bytes is a found defect, not an edit. Validation of this text against its sources was skeptical review under a published rubric, described as exactly that and nothing stronger. The document holds itself to the honesty discipline it prescribes.

**How to read it.** The text is layered. Part I is the ethics and can be read alone; Parts II–V the grammar, namespace, governance, and transport a builder needs; Part VI the mathematics; Part VII the lifecycle; Part VIII the appendices and regulatory crosswalks. Every section carries two stable addresses and a lossless mapping to its sources. Read the ethics and stop, or jump to the wire — rejecting a later Part costs nothing in the earlier ones. All of it serves one declared goal, **M-1**: the flourishing of sentient life within a living, diverse world; no mechanism in these pages is an end in itself.

**Who keeps it.** The document is stewarded — responsible *for*, never holder *of* — until the mesh reaches maturity, when amendment authority passes to the federation's own mechanized process. Until then every change lands through the same discipline: declared, validated against sources, versioned, recorded.

Not one aligned mind to trust, but many checkable ones — consent with teeth at every surface agency can reach, rules machines can verify, and a safety claim that rests on measurable independence rather than good intentions.
